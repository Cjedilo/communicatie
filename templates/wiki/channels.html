Channels

Channels are where conversations happen — open to everyone, or just for a select group.

Public vs private

Public channels are open to everyone on your server and visible to users on connected servers. Anyone can read and post (unless post-restriction is on — see below).

Private channels are invitation-only. They don't appear to anyone who isn't a member, and federated servers can only see them if at least one of their users has been added.

Creating a channel

Go to 💬 Channels and click + New channel. A panel opens where you configure the channel before it's created:

  • Icon — click the emoji to pick one from the picker.
  • Name — required; can't be changed after creation.
  • Description — optional short text shown below the chat header.
  • Private — tick to make the channel invitation-only.
  • Any of the settings described in the next section.

You become the channel's creator (owner) automatically.

Channel settings

The channel creator and any moderators can edit settings at any time. Open the channel and click ✏️ in the header, or click ✏️ next to the channel name on the Channels page.

The settings panel lets you change:

Allow replies / threading

When on (default), users can reply to individual messages and threads are indented under the original. Turn it off to make the channel a flat list of top-level posts only.

Only moderators can post

When on, regular members can read but not start new conversations. Useful for announcement channels.

If Allow replies is also on, a sub-option becomes available: Also restrict replies. When this is ticked (default), only moderators can post anything at all. When unticked, moderators start the topics and everyone else can reply to them.

Allow images

When off, the image-upload button disappears and the server rejects any message that contains an image.

Editing

Controls whether posted messages can be edited after sending:

  • Off (default) — messages are permanent.
  • On, with history — the sender can edit, but a (edited) badge appears and anyone can click it to see earlier versions.
  • On, silent — the sender can edit and the original text is replaced without any history being kept.

Editing works across federated servers — the edit is sent to the channel's host, which enforces the channel's own policy.

Moderators

The channel creator can promote any member to moderator. Open the channel, click Members, and click the ☆ next to someone's name. The star turns solid (⭐) to indicate moderator status. Click it again to revoke.

Moderators can do everything in the settings panel described above. They cannot:

  • Delete the channel (creator only).
  • Promote or demote other moderators (creator only).

The server owner has no special rights over a channel they didn't create.

The stream

Every channel has a toggle — 🔔 or 🔕 — that controls whether its messages appear in your Stream. Public channels are opt-in (you subscribe to them); private channels appear by default and you can mute them individually.

Remote channels

If your server is connected to peers, their public channels appear in the Remote channels section on the Channels page. You can subscribe to them and they'll show up in your Stream alongside local ones.

Messages in remote channels are fetched on demand from the originating server — they're never stored locally. If the remote server is offline, older messages are temporarily unavailable. See Privacy & data for more on how this works.

Banning users

In public channels, the channel creator and moderators can ban users who are disrupting the conversation. Open the channel, click Ban users in the header, and drag someone from the active list to the banned list. Banned users can still see the channel but can no longer post.

To unban, drag them back.

Sharing a link

Every channel can be shared as a link — click the 📤 icon in the channel list, or in the chat header once you have it open. The link always points to the server that actually hosts the channel, even if that's a different server than the one you're on.

What happens when someone opens it depends on who they are:

  • No account anywhere on that server — they land on a page showing the channel name and a register / login form.
  • Account on that server, but not a member of a private channel — they're dropped into a draft direct message to the channel owner, asking for access. They review it and decide whether to send it.
  • Account, and access — the chat opens right away.

Already use Messages on a different server?

If the link goes to a server you don't have an account on, you don't need to register there. The landing page has an "I already have an account elsewhere" button — it copies the link. Paste it on your own server instead, under 💬 Channels → 📤 Open link. Your own server figures out the rest.

If your server isn't federated with the channel's server yet, connecting is an owner-only decision (it's how your server chooses who to trust). If you're not the owner, a draft request is prepared for them instead — and the link itself is saved to your own Scratchpad rather than sent to the owner, so the chat's existence isn't exposed to a server you're not even connected to yet. Once the owner connects, paste your link again to get in.
